Tuesday night, jittery with the anticipation of his confrontation in Denmark the following day, to get to sleep Hampton took a couple of Unisom, along with a couple of shots of Hiram's whiskey. As was often the case, sleeping under that sort of influence made his dreams especially vivid and strange. 

*************

Hampton is sitting in a row of seats in an almost completely dark room. Suddenly a spotlight kicks on, and he sees that Hiram is standing on the edge of a stage a few rows in front of him. Hiram declaims, as if an old timey actor, "Tonight we are presenting your life, parts one, two, and three. Just try not to be a dick, 'cause your errors ain't funny."

He waves his arms as if casting a spell, and Hampton finds himself standing in the middle of Magnolia Avenue. Looking up through a light purple rain, he sees Camellia on the roof of Elsie's, singing down to him:

Do you remember that hot summer’s day?
Goin' to Finley Park, almost a date.
We took a ride in Ole Sikes' truck of hay,
Bouncin' all around like we was mere freight.
We was all tired so we did a few lines,
Lovin' the relief from a little wind;
Rockin' to the music, beneath the pines,
If we was havin' fun, that mean we sinned?
I'm not carin' if I don't make the grade
Not your possession, but you don't listen,
Ignore me, sure, but I ain't throwin' shade,
You can spit in the wind, but don't try pissin'.
    Long as I can breathe and my eyes can see,
    I love you still but Imma stay free.

As she finishes her song, three older women, in black, hooded capes, appear alongside her. They shout down to Hampton that their names are Revenge, Rape, and Murder. They drag Camellia back from the edge of the roof, and Hampton hears her cry for help as she disappears. He rushes through the door of the bar, aiming to get up on the roof to save her.

But instead of the bar, Hampton find himself inside a circus tent.
Claude is in the center ring, dressed as a lion tamer and holding a whip and a chair. But instead of a lion, the animal he is trying to tame is a small rodent. When Hampton looks closer, he sees the creature has Gertie's face. But rather than becoming tamer, the rodent grows in size and then dances around the cage, first singing "My Sharona," then, "Don't Fear the Reaper." 

The Gertie-rodent begins to flap her front paws as she prances about the ring. The paws gradually fan out, and violin rivers of golden dust trail behind them. They are the translucent, iridescent wings of a moth, and the moth lifts up in flight, pulling Hampton with it. Up through a hole in the center of the circus tent, and out into splintered sunlight they rise. A chorus somewhere in the distance sings:

O man, you sure done it now
Alastair Macquistador.
You gonna be scraped outta
 Gray pigstye for this.
That cat, that holds his
 Regex spinning on a letterbox,
Which will be piped through poppycock.
 In the ninth inning. A Macquistador, but
 Afraid to speak? Run away in shame, Alastair!

A very confused Hampton gradually found himself lowered into a clearing in a forest of snow-laden fir trees. Far off in the distance, he thought he saw something like a wild cat on the prowl. As he began to trudge through the snow, he found Hiram by his side, whispering in his ear "You're being a dick for the second time."

Hampton felt like an ass. What was he doing in this snowy forest anyway? He had to make like a hockey player and get the puck out of here!  He turned to Hiram to tell him as much, but his friend had vanished. 

As he looked around to see where Hiram had gone, there slowly emerged through the lacy filigree on the branches of the firs some sad, old men. 

"We run this town," they told him in unison.

"What town are you talking about? We're out in the middle of the woods." 

"Is that what you think? Take a look around you."

Hampton did so and saw that what he thought had been fir trees were now the pillars holding up the roof of a courtroom. The courtroom was packed with people. Hampton was surprised to find that Claude was the judge, wearing one of those white wigs like he was a judge in England, and he called Hampton to approach the bench.

Claude leaned forward and whispered to him, "My lack of mercy ain't strained by havin' you killed. Imma rain violence down on you two ways: right on your ass, and on anyone who stands up for you. Now you git on back to your seat."

Upon returning to his seat, Hampton found that Hiram was apparently his defense attorney. He leaned over in his turn and said confidentially to Hampton, "This whole trial is a whole lotta fuss about nothin'."

Hampton asked him if he were sure about that, and Hiram nodded yes. He continued:

"Look, you ain't no hero. You look like a lion but you roar like a lamb. You wearin' that badge of bitterness like you think it a badge of honor. You a hollow man, and you change friends like you change hats."

Hiram begins to laugh while repeatedly tilting his head back. But each time he does so, his laugh sounds more and more like a cackle, and his beak grows long and pointed. His dark skin becomes black feathers. He is a crow sitting on a tree in a graveyard. In the mist below the tree, a dog is barking up at that crow. 

A shrouded figure moves through the tombstones, fleetingly illuminated when the full moon peeks out from behind the dark clouds. When the figure comes closer, Hampton realizes that it is his father. 

"I need you to see something, my son."

He takes off the cap he was wearing. Hampton sees that it was hiding a pair of horns. His father lowers his head and charges at Hampton, horns first.

Just as he is about to be gored, he spins away, right back into the courtroom, where the jury is delivering its verdict. Instead of the foreman reading out the entire result, each juror stands and declares their verdict about the case. To Hampton's surprise, Hiram was no longer next to him as his attorney, but instead standing in the jury box.  When the judge asked for Hiram's verdict, he replied, "Guilty!"

Hampton looked at his friend with disbelief. "You too?"

Claude repeatedly banging his gavel ordered him to shut up. Maxwell's silver hammer, and it came down upon his head, sending him reeling. When he came to, he was in an ancient jail cell. A Shriner Clown stood just beyond the bars, looking in at him.

 Hampton: This cell is very dark. 

Clown: Whachoo sayin'? We in a church, and the Lord's light be pour-in' through the stained glass windows.

Hampton: I am not mad. This cell is damned dark.

Clown: If you think it be dark, maybe the dark is from your eyes? 

Hampton: Man, you jes playin' me for ignorant. Ain't nobody never been abused like me. 

Clown: What is the opinion of Beyoncé concerning ducks?

Hampton: That the soul of my grandma might be a bird on the wing.

Clown:  You think she right?

Hampton: My grandma ain't no duck.

Clown: Okay, you have it your way. You ain't got the sense God gave a goose, so you just stay in yo' dark cell.

[The clown walks away, but in walks Norbert from the gospel group. ]

Hampton: Norbert, whachoo you doin' here? 

Norbert: I come to help you find your own voice, and bring the Word to the people. 

As do redeemers, Norbert slowly too fades away. 

A wagon follows behind, loaded with clay.

And the seeds that were silent burst into concernancy.

Hampton is in the back seat of a 56 Chevy, speeding north along I26. Alastair and Thaddeus are in the front, discussing the weather.

Alastair said, "Man, it is one hot, cold, sultry day!"

Thaddeus added, "And clear and stormy!"

They take the turn off for Arden, just a few miles south of Asheville. They pull up along the forested shores of Lake Julian, and stop the car. They simultaneously turn around to address Hampton. "We gotta go piss," they tell him in unison.

As they stand on the bank, peeing into the lake, Alastair says, "Man, this water cold."

Thaddeus replies, "Yeah, and it deep too!"

At that moment, two giant alligators lunge forward out of the mist-covered lake, grab the two men, and drag them beneath the coal black waters. Hampton could have done nothing to save them. And now their bodies will be left in the lake to rot and rot.

Hampton climbs into the front seat of the car, and turns the key in the ignition. The radio comes on as the car starts. There is singing:

The more my brother looks like me
The less I understand
This silent war
That's bloodied both our hands

As Hampton drives away from the lake, he passes, cavorting in the grassy verge: 

- a spellbound Irish rat fleeing a bard, 

- a management meeting of horses, 

- a doe parading with her fawns

- a three-legged hog, munching on a corn husk.

The country road turned into a major interstate. The sign said he was heading towards Augusta. He took the exit for Windsor, and found himself in the middle of a protest march. His progress was blocked, so he pulled over to the side of the road, parked, and got out of his car. 

He was curious what the protest was about. When he asked participants, what he learned was that the protesters were protesting against the leaders of the previous protest, which had been staged against the leaders of the protest before that one. 

After he talks with a number of them, the protesters decide that he is okay, and they will let him pass. He gets back in his car, thinking, "All's well that ends well."

But as he tries to put the car into drive, it seems that the stick shift and steering wheel have vanished. Instead, there is a table before him, and chairs to his sides. The chairs are occupied by Claude and Gertie,  who are chatting, seemingly not aware that he is there.

"What's that on your dress?" Claude asked. 

"A spot of blood from when I cut my finger the other day. It just won't come out."

Behind the bar, Agnes and Donna are stirring what appears to be a large jar of fruit punch.
